What is BATTLEGROUND?

Lassonde Innovation Battleground is a competition designed to encourage Lassonde School of Engineering and Schulich School of Business students to collaborate and solve problems currently faced by the engineering industry. It is a unique platform with countless opportunities where students can design solutions and build startups; improve their skillset and much more!

This competition also simulates real life challenges, which may be faced by students once they start working in their respective fields. Apart from providing students with hands on experience, we will be inviting a panel of experts representing different fields of the engineering industry to evaluate the performance of our talented students.

Real World Problems

Deal with the scaled down versions of various problems- currently faced by the engineering industry.

The Experts are Here

Learn from and talk to industry experts and York faculty who will host workshops during the competitions.

Evaluation

Get your performance evaluated by a panel of experts representing different fields of the engineering industry.

The Event Format

What Happens at what times.


DESIGN
Teams are required to create an architectural structure/design layout with brief explanation of their solution. While designing, each team will be allowed 30 minutes of expert advise from members of the consultant panel.

CONSULTANT APPROVAL
A panel of graduate students or alumni will be formed to inspect the design layout presented by the teams. The approval will be based on categories like feasibility of the solution, safety standards etc. A design will be approved if a layout meets the minimum requirements of the categories listed above. Consultants will enlist the areas of concerns and provide valuable feedback to fix the design.

PREPARING A PITCH
Teams will be required to present their design to the judges panel. Teams will have the liberty to use any medium possible. Each team will be allowed 30 minutes of expert advise from members of the consultant panel.

PROTOYPING
Teams will receive materials to construct their prototypes of their design in the allotted time. Please check the schedule for more information.

TESTING N DEBUGGING
Each team will be allotted separate time slots to test their designs. The duration depends on the complexity of the problem.

PITCHING
All teams will present their design in front of Judges. This panel may consist any combination of Industry Professionals and Professors.

WORKSHOPS

We offer workshops through our Industrial Insight program. These workshops are specifically designed to help students improve their performance in battleground. Some of the workshops offered by Industrial Insight program this year are “3D Printing”, “How to manage your resources”, “My battle with Raspberry Pi”, Machine shop Training and many more.
